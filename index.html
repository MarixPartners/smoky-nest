Mountain Coasters 🎢

Smoky Mountain Alpine Coaster

Coaster · 1.3 mi — closest!

Our nearest coaster and one of the longest downhill rides in the U.S. — over a mile of gravity-driven track through the forest, with rider-controlled brakes and speeds up to 27 mph. Open day and night.

Where: 867 Wears Valley Rd, Pigeon Forge
~$18 adult
Directions Website

Rocky Top Mountain Coaster

Coaster · across from Dollywood

Voted Pigeon Forge's #1 mountain coaster (2022–2025) and the longest in East Tennessee. Sprawls over 20 acres with tunnels, 360° turns, four uplifts, and speeds near 30 mph.

Where: 2940 Veterans Blvd, Pigeon Forge
$18 ride · $9 re-ride
Directions Website

Pigeon Forge Racing Coaster

Coaster · on the Parkway

The world's only racing mountain coaster — dual side-by-side tracks let you race family and friends downhill at rider-controlled speeds up to 30 mph.

On: The Parkway, Pigeon Forge
Single rides ~$18–20
Directions Website

Rowdy Bear's Mountain

Coaster & Glider · 2.8 mi

Home of the Wild Stallion Mountain Coaster plus the area's first Alpine Mountain Glider — a dual side-by-side zip-style ride. Year-round snow tubing too. A close, action-packed stop.

On: The Parkway, Pigeon Forge

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Cades Cove Loop

Scenic Drive · ~29 mi

An 11-mile one-way loop through open valley framed by mountains — historic cabins, churches, and the area's best wildlife viewing (deer, bears, turkeys).

Tip: Go early to beat traffic; bring binoculars.
Free · parking tag req'd
Directions Park Info

Smoky Mtns Parking Tag

Good to Know · —

There's no entrance fee to the park, but you need a parking tag to stop for 15+ minutes. Grab one online or at a visitor center before you hike.

Buy at: Sugarlands & Cades Cove visitor centers
$5 day · $15 week · $40 year
